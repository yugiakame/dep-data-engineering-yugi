## Learner Support

### Starter Script / Template

Use this write-up frame for one careful inference section:

```md
## Inference Check

### Relationship or Comparison
I am checking whether <group_a> differs from <group_b> on <your_metric>.

### What the Data Shows
<one or two sentences describing the observed pattern>

### What I Can Say Carefully
<plain-language interpretation without claiming causality>

### What I Cannot Conclude
<possible confounders, limits, or missing context>
```

### How To Adapt This To Your Project

- Choose one relationship that matters to the original question.
- Keep the comparison understandable even if you do not run a formal test.
- State limits clearly if the data is observational or incomplete.

### Definition of Done

- You analyze one meaningful comparison or relationship.
- The interpretation is cautious and evidence-based.
- You separate observation from conclusion.
- You explicitly avoid unsupported causal claims.

### Common Mistakes

- Treating correlation as proof of causation.
- Writing "this caused that" when the data cannot support it.
- Running a test without explaining what it means in plain language.
- Forgetting to mention uncertainty or data limits.

### If You’re Stuck After 2 Hours

- Write what you observed first, then write one sentence about what you cannot conclude.
- Use simple group comparisons if formal inference feels too advanced.
- Ask whether a decision-maker would misread your wording as stronger evidence than it is.
